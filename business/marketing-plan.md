# Marketing Plan
## Positioning
### Primary Positioning Statement
"Empower indie iOS developers to create stunning App Store preview videos with ease, without requiring extensive video editing knowledge."

### Alternative Positioning Statements
1. "Unlock your App Store potential with our intuitive video creation tool, designed specifically for indie iOS developers."
2. "Say goodbye to video editing headaches and hello to professional-looking App Store preview videos with app-preview-craft."
3. "Streamline your App Store preview video creation process with our user-friendly, template-based solution."

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
### Persona 1: Alex Chen
* Daily Job: iOS App Developer at a small startup
* Budget: $500/month
* Pain Points: Difficulty creating high-quality App Store preview videos, limited video editing skills
* Goals: Increase app visibility, improve user engagement

### Persona 2: Maya Patel
* Daily Job: Freelance iOS App Developer
* Budget: $1,000/month
* Pain Points: Limited time to create App Store preview videos, difficulty standing out in a crowded market
* Goals: Increase app downloads, improve reputation as a developer

### Persona 3: Ethan Kim
* Daily Job: iOS App Developer at a mid-sized company
* Budget: $2,000/month
* Pain Points: Difficulty creating consistent App Store preview videos, limited resources for video editing
* Goals: Improve app visibility, increase user engagement

## Channels
### Top 3 Channels with CAC Estimate
1. **Social Media** (CAC: $10-$20): Leverage platforms like Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook to reach indie iOS developers, share engaging content, and drive traffic to the app-preview-craft website.
2. **Content Marketing** (CAC: $20-$50): Create informative blog posts, videos, and guides that address the pain points of indie iOS developers, positioning app-preview-craft as a solution.
3. **Influencer Marketing** (CAC: $50-$100): Partner with popular iOS development influencers to promote app-preview-craft to their followers, increasing brand awareness and driving sign-ups.

## Content Cadence
### Week-by-Week Content Plan
Week 1-2: Introduction to app-preview-craft, benefits of using the tool
Week 3-4: Tips and tricks for creating high-quality App Store preview videos
Week 5-6: Success stories and case studies from existing users
Week 7-8: Behind-the-scenes look at the app-preview-craft development process
Week 9-10: Exclusive discounts and promotions for early adopters

## Launch Milestones
### D-30 (30 Days Before Launch)
* Finalize marketing materials, including website, social media, and content assets
* Establish influencer partnerships and content calendar
* Launch pre-launch email campaign to build anticipation

### D-0 (Launch Day)
* Launch app-preview-craft website, social media, and content channels
* Send launch email campaign to subscribers
* Host a live webinar to introduce the tool and answer questions

### D+30 (30 Days After Launch)
* Analyze launch metrics, identify areas for improvement
* Launch a referral program to encourage word-of-mouth marketing
* Host a live Q&A session to address user concerns

### D+90 (90 Days After Launch)
* Review 90-day metrics, adjust marketing strategy as needed
* Launch a case study campaign to showcase app-preview-craft success stories
* Host a live webinar to discuss future development plans and feature requests

## Success Metrics
### DAU (Daily Active Users)
* Target: 100 DAU within the first 30 days
* Metric: Track daily user engagement, including login frequency and feature usage

### $MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
* Target: $10,000 MRR within the first 90 days
* Metric: Track monthly subscription revenue, adjusting pricing as needed to meet targets
